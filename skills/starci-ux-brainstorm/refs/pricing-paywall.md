## Pricing, Paywall & Upgrade — Reference for StarCi Academy

---

### Dominant patterns

**1. Soft freemium gate: content preview, then hard stop**

The near-universal model. Let users taste value (first module, N lessons, N quiz attempts), then block at a logical continuation point — not mid-sentence. The gate fires at a "success moment" (lesson completed, module finished) rather than on page load. Conversion after 3 completed modules is measurably higher than on first contact.

- Coursera (Aug 2025): replaced lifetime audit with "Preview mode" — free access to Module 1 only, including graded assignments and AI Coach, then paywall. Tight, clear, generous enough to feel value.
- Brilliant.org: all courses browseable, first few interactive problems free, then lock — but the lock surfaces with a preview of the next problem (curiosity gap, not brick wall).
- Codecademy: free "Basic" tier covers introductory lessons; Career Paths, quizzes, projects, certificates all behind Pro. Lock icon on sidebar items.

**2. Friction-as-conversion: the Hearts / Energy mechanic**

Duolingo's most-studied pattern. Instead of a content gate, they gate *progress*: free users get 5 hearts (or 25 energy since July 2025), depleted by mistakes or exercise completions respectively. Running out triggers a hard stop with an upgrade modal or mandatory ad. Seven premium entry points exist within a single session (shop, hearts view, home banner, post-lesson ad screen, streak store, review tab, profile). This works at Duolingo's scale (4.2M paying subs, 47% YoY revenue increase 2023) but risks community backlash — their energy system change caused mass Reddit criticism.

**3. Annual/monthly toggle — default to annual, show savings explicitly**

Industry standard for SaaS pricing pages. Defaulting to annual increases annual plan adoption by ~19%. Show the monthly-equivalent of the annual price, crossed-out monthly price next to it, and a badge ("Save 40%"). Toggle position: directly below the hero headline, above the plan cards.

- Coursera Plus: $399/yr or $59/mo. Currently 40% off to $240 for new subs.
- Brilliant: $161.88/yr ($13.49/mo equiv) vs $27.99/mo — a 52% annual discount, prominently displayed.
- Duolingo Super: $83.99/yr vs $12.99/mo — 46% annual discount.
- DataCamp: defaults toggle to annual ("Save $255 with Yearly"), shows monthly-equiv price.

**4. Three-tier plan structure with a highlighted "recommended" tier**

Pricing pages with 4+ tiers convert 31% worse than 3-tier. One plan visually distinguished: slightly elevated card, colored border, "Most Popular" or "Best Value" badge. Pages without a highlighted tier convert 22% worse.

Common tier shapes:
- **Free / Pro / Team** (Codecademy-style)
- **Free / Plus / Max** (Coursera-style)
- **Free / Premium / Family** (Duolingo, Brilliant)

**5. Feature-gating by accountability/credential, not by content quantity**

Counterintuitively, gating raw content access underperforms vs. gating:
- Certificates & credentials (users pay for the proof, not the knowledge)
- AI feedback speed (free = 24-hour delay, paid = instant)
- Streak protection and progress safety nets
- Cross-device sync / offline access
- Usage caps (N AI queries/month, N quiz retries)

This is what separates platforms that convert from platforms that frustrate.

**6. Contextual paywall modal (not a redirect)**

Best practice: trigger an upgrade modal or bottom sheet in-context when the user hits a locked action — do not navigate away to /pricing. The modal shows: what they tried to do, what they'd unlock, plan options (simplified — not full comparison), and a single primary CTA. Duolingo customizes the first bullet of the paywall copy to match the feature that triggered it.

**7. Trial over hard freemium for complex products**

Free trials in EdTech convert at ~22% vs 5–8% for open freemium. For products with setup complexity (AI tutor, code execution, multi-course paths), 7–14 days is optimal. 80%+ of conversions happen on Day 0 — so the trial start screen is as important as the pricing page.

**8. Social proof — quantified metrics over testimonials**

"Trusted by 1M learners" outperforms generic quotes. "Recognizable logos increase trust scores 47%." Place social proof immediately below the headline or above the pricing cards — not at page bottom. Video testimonials convert 29% better than text-only.

**9. Annual discount urgency — real, not fake**

Ethical urgency: limited-time promotional rates with a real end date (Coursera's 40% off to $240 is time-boxed). Avoid artificial countdown timers — "damages trust faster than no urgency." Flash sales: Udemy's model historically used aggressive flash pricing, but their CFO acknowledged the pivot to subscriptions at 85% revenue share.

**10. Khan Academy counter-pattern: no paywall, donation ask**

Khan Academy runs 100% free, no paywall, donor-funded. Their donate page uses copy like "Free to Use. Not Free to Make." with aspirational impact framing. Less than 1% of users donate. They added Khanmigo (AI tutor) as a paid add-on in 2023, keeping core free. Reference: the pure mission-driven model is viable at scale with grant funding, not without it.

### Concrete examples per platform

**Coursera**
- Aug 2025 "Preview mode": Module 1 free (including graded assignments + AI Coach), then paywall. Hard shift from lifetime audit.
- Plan: individual courses $14.99–$99 or Coursera Plus at $59/mo / $399/yr (currently $240 promotional).
- 7,000+ courses in Plus. 14-day money-back guarantee on annual.
- Freemium-to-subscription play: audit → preview is a tighter funnel.
- Enterprise plan: Coursera for Teams from ~$399/user/yr.

**Duolingo**
- Free tier: 5 hearts (mistakes drain them) → now 25 energy units (every exercise drains them, July 2025). Ads after each lesson. No offline. Streak vulnerable.
- Super Duolingo: unlimited hearts, no ads, offline, streak repair, progress boosts. $12.99/mo or $83.99/yr.
- Family Plan: $119.99/yr, up to 6 accounts.
- 7+ upgrade entry points per session.
- Contextual paywall copy matches trigger feature.
- 4.2M paying subscribers.

**Brilliant.org**
- Free tier: first few problems in each course, limited daily.
- Premium ($13.49/mo annual / $27.99/mo): unlimited courses, Koji AI tutor, no ads.
- Family plan: fixed price, up to 6 members.
- Trial: offered periodically (not always-on).
- Paywall inside course: visual trial timeline (trust-building), testimonials, feature comparison table.

**Codecademy**
- Free ("Basic"): intro lessons in "Free Course" labeled courses only.
- Plus: ~$14.99/mo annual — quizzes, practice content, projects.
- Pro: ~$39.99/mo — all of Plus + AI tools, job-readiness content.
- Student discount: up to 50% off.
- Lock icon on sidebar for Pro content. Some users report confusion about what exactly is gated.

**edX**
- Audit track: free, time-limited (some courses restrict to 1–2 weeks), no graded assignments, no certificate.
- Verified Certificate: $50–$300 per course.
- Professional Certificate programs: $200–$2,500.
- MicroMasters/MicroBachelors: $600–$1,500.
- Since 2U acquisition (2021), free access has tightened. Community backlash noted.

**Udemy**
- Per-course model: $14.99–$199.99 per course, heavy discount cadence.
- Udemy Business subscription: teams. Instructor payout dropped to 15% of subscription revenue (Jan 2026).
- CFO stated: intentionally reducing single-course sales to push subscriptions.
- Flash sale model still runs (regional pricing + behavior-triggered coupons).
- No freemium within courses — preview is trailers only (1–3 free preview videos per course).

### DO / DON'T heuristics

**DO:**
- Gate at "success moments" — after a module, after a meaningful action, not on page load.
- Default the annual/monthly toggle to annual; show explicit savings ("Save 46% — billed yearly").
- Use a 3-tier structure. Visually highlight the middle (recommended) tier with a colored ring or "Popular" badge.
- Trigger upgrade as a modal/bottom sheet in-context — keep user on the page they were on.
- Personalize the paywall headline to the triggering feature ("Unlock unlimited AI hints").
- Use quantified social proof above the fold — learner count, courses, completion rate.
- Show "Cancel anytime" and a money-back guarantee near the CTA — refund guarantees increase conversion 12–18% even when rarely used.
- Gate AI credits, streak shields, certificates, and instant feedback — not raw content access.
- Frame annual pricing in daily cost ("Less than a coffee per week").
- Offer a free trial (7–14 days) for your most complex features — EdTech trials convert at ~22% vs 5–8% freemium.
- Make lock icons visible but non-hostile — show a teaser/preview of locked content, not a blank card.
- Stack pricing tiers vertically on mobile; horizontal card rows fail on narrow viewports.

**DON'T:**
- Navigate away to /pricing when a paywall triggers mid-flow. Kill the momentum.
- Show 4+ pricing tiers — conversion drops 31%.
- Use artificial countdown timers or fake scarcity — damages trust.
- Spam the upgrade ask during onboarding — one clear ask, max 3 prompts per session.
- Gate basic educational content behind paywall and leave free tier hollow — causes backlash (edX) and high churn.
- Show a banner, a modal, AND a sticky CTA simultaneously — visual collision.
- Use vague CTA copy ("Learn more", "Upgrade"). Use outcome copy: "Unlock all challenges", "Start free trial".
- Hide the price until the last step.
- Make cancellation hard to find — triggers payment processor disputes and trust destruction.
- Replicate Duolingo's energy/hearts mechanic without their scale and community buy-in — high risk of revolt.

### Takeaways for StarCi

StarCi's model (freemium → paid, courses → modules → lessons/challenges, AI co-pilot, XP/leagues/streaks, gamified) maps well to several patterns above. Specific recommendations:

**Freemium gate placement**
- Free: first module of every course, full access. Lock at module 2+ for enrolled users without a plan.
- Alternatively (lower friction): audit full course content, but gate certificates, AI co-pilot credits, challenge submissions, and streak shields. This is the "credential + accountability" gating pattern that outperforms content gating.

**Paywall UI (in-context modal)**
- Trigger: modal/sheet, stays on the current lesson/content page.
- Above fold: one-line outcome headline ("Keep your streak going" / "Unlock AI hints for every challenge").
- Feature list: 3–4 bullets max, benefit-framed, with teal checkmarks.
- Plan options: annual default, toggle to monthly. Show "Save 40%" badge on annual.
- Single primary CTA: "Start 7-day free trial" (if offering trial) or "Unlock Pro".
- Below CTA: "Cancel anytime · No commitment" in muted text.
- Social proof: learner count or "4.8/5 from 2,000+ learners" near top.

**Pricing page (/pricing)**
- 3 tiers: Free / Pro / Team (or Free / Pro / Unlimited). Pro highlighted.
- Annual toggle above the cards, defaulting to annual.
- Feature table: outcomes-framed ("Unlimited AI co-pilot hints", "All challenges + solutions", "Weekly league ranking protection"), not feature names.
- Social proof strip: total learners, courses, XP milestones.
- Mobile: vertical stack, collapsible comparison table.

**AI co-pilot (credit pool)**
- The unified credit pool (per existing BE architecture) fits cleanly as a plan differentiator: Free = 5 AI uses/week, Pro = unlimited. Show a usage counter in the UI ("3 of 5 AI credits remaining this week") with a subtle "Upgrade for unlimited" link — not a modal until they actually exhaust credits.
- Never show two bars (auto vs premium lane) — per the `credit-unified-pool-ui` rule already in the repo.

**Gamification + freemium**
- Streak shields / league protection: exclusively Pro. Free users see their streak risk ("Your 14-day streak is at risk — streak shields are a Pro feature"). This is Duolingo's accountability gating pattern without the hostile energy drain.
- Weekly leagues: free users can see the leaderboard, but cannot use streak repair or XP boosts. Nudge copy on league view: "Pro members used 2× XP boosts this week."
- Badges + certificates: earned badges visible to all, official completion certificates gated to Pro.

**Annual toggle specifics**
- Default to annual in the pricing UI.
- Show: "Pro Annual — 12,000 ₫/day · billed 4,380,000 ₫/year" + crossed-out monthly price.
- "Save 40%" badge on the annual tab.
- For StarCi's VN market: consider monthly as default if cash-flow sensitivity is high for the target demographic (students), and use the trial to bridge.

**Trial**
- 7-day free trial of Pro, no credit card if technically feasible, clearly states billing date.
- Trial start screen = important as pricing page. Show what they're unlocking, not what the trial is.
- Follow-up: Day 5 in-app nudge "2 days left in your Pro trial — keep your challenge streaks going."

**Color + motion (HeroUI v3 / teal accent)**
- Recommended plan card: teal #00a898 border ring, "Most Popular" badge in teal on white pill.
- Lock icons: use a minimal padlock SVG in `text-default-400` (muted), not red — red feels punitive. On hover: slide reveal of teaser content behind the lock.
- Upgrade CTA button: solid teal, white text, `shadow-sm`. Not outline.
- Annual savings badge: `bg-success/10 text-success` (per the chip color convention already in the repo).
- Toggle: HeroUI Switch or segmented tab, teal active state.
- Paywall modal: `backdrop-blur-sm` overlay, card max-w-md, centered, no full-screen takeover on desktop.
